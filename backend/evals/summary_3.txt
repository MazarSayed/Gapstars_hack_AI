Meeting Purpose

Clarify career trajectory logic and define platform architecture.

Key Takeaways

  - Career Trajectory UI: Overlapping roles will be displayed with the longest-duration experience as primary, using visual offsets and a dynamic timeline to show concurrency.
  - Experience Calculation: Total Experience is the span from first job start to last job end (minus breaks >6 months). Industry Experience is the sum of time in each industry, calculated separately.
  - Data Source Strategy: Shift from unreliable LinkedIn scraping to AI (Grok) for company data (industry, summary) to ensure accuracy, especially for senior profiles and startups.
  - SaaS Architecture: Implement login/authentication now to enable a multi-client SaaS model with shared core data and client-specific summaries, avoiding a costly future refactor.

Topics

Career Trajectory UI & Logic

  - Problem: The UI fails to represent parallel work experiences, a common scenario for senior candidates.
  - Solution: A new UI and data logic will be implemented.
      - Primary Experience: For any overlapping period, the longest-duration role becomes the primary display.
      - Visual Hierarchy:
          - Primary role card → Aligned to the left.
          - Overlapping role cards → Offset to the right to visually signal concurrency.
      - Dynamic Timeline: Clicking a role card highlights its specific duration on the timeline in blue.
      - Ordering:
          - Current roles ("present") → Ordered by total years at the company.
          - Past roles → Ordered chronologically by start date.
  - Bug Fix: The missing "from-to" dates for current roles will be fixed in the next release.

Experience Calculation Logic

  - Total Years of Experience:
      - Calculated as the span from the first job's start date to the last job's end date (or present).
      - Breaks >6 months → Deducted from the total.
      - Breaks <6 months → Ignored.
  - Years of Industry Experience:
      - Calculated separately by summing time in each industry from individual jobs.
      - Rationale: This accounts for experience across multiple industries, so the total will not necessarily match the Total Years of Experience.
  - UI Feedback:
      - Mazar: Unlink the auto-update between Total and Industry Experience fields.
      - Swarnaka: Start all industry experience rings from the 12 o'clock position for visual consistency.

Data Source & Summarization Strategy

  - Problem: LinkedIn data is unreliable for company industry and information, especially for senior profiles and startups.
  - Solution: Use AI (Grok) for company data enrichment.
      - Process: Scrape only the company name from LinkedIn; use Grok to find and summarize company information (industry, description).
      - Rationale: Grok pulls from a broader range of sources, providing more accurate and detailed data.
  - Candidate Title: The title displayed next to the candidate's picture will be the title from the primary role (the longest-duration role) on the career trajectory.

SaaS Architecture & Authentication

  - Decision: Implement login/authentication now, before the backend becomes more complex.
  - Rationale:
      - Enables SaaS Model: Supports a multi-client platform with separate, secure databases.
      - Prevents Costly Refactor: Building authentication later would require a major data structure overhaul.
      - Data Security: Ensures compliance with standards like GDPR.
  - Data Structure: The architecture will support two data types:
      - Core Data: Shared across clients (e.g., raw LinkedIn profiles) to reduce enrichment costs.
      - Summarized Data: Client-specific (e.g., AI-generated career summaries).
  - Initial Accounts: Create two accounts for testing: one for Celestial and one for HNC.

Process Improvement: Wireframe Reviews

  - New Process: Before each sprint, hold a dedicated Q&A session with Lulu and Oshady to review wireframes and clarify requirements.
  - Timing: The team will decide if this review happens before or after sprint planning.
